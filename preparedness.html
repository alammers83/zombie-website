Preparedness

If the world ended tomorrow, what would you do? Would you be prepared?

Chances are, no. Follow some of these guidelines to get you started:

If your evacuation or shelter in place plan includes more than just you, make sure you know where to meet the other members of your party.

Visit the Red Cross for more info.

Stock up now, because when the zombie apocalypse comes, stores are going to be swarming with people clambering for supplies. Meanwhile, you get to sit back and avoid leaving your fortified shelter.

Sterilize everything that has come into contact with the infected with bleach with bleach and wear gloves.

Protect your nose, mouth eyes and any cuts and scrapes from zombie fluids.

What you wear is about as important as what you are carrying with you. You don't want zombies to be able drag you down or take a bite of your sweet human flesh. Make sure if you have to go out, you have yourself covered.

Wear a sturdy leather jacket, pants and gloves when outside your fortified shelter. It'll offer you some protection against bites. Don't wear loose fitting clothing that zombies can grab hold of.

You can use duct tape to patch anything and it's hard to bite through. (This goes for gear and pretty much anything else. Stock up on a lot of duct tape.)

If you have long hair, cut it or keep it tied up and not able to be grabbed.


Keep your home, bags and bug out bags ready to go!
